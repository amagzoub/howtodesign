Week 2 - Context and Culture

This week we were assigned to choose the context that we want to work in. I had two competing ideas:

anatomy of desk lamps vs. basement lighting.


Desk Lamp Anatomy

I wanted to identify the basic components of a lamp were. After which, I would compare across different lamps and observe the similarities and differences between these designs. Below is my research and findings.

Sketch of lamp structure

A basic desk lamp has three main components: the head, neck and base. The design of these three interfaces depend on the design intent, function and form language expressed and set by the designer.

Below are three images of different lamps. At first sight, they look very different, however the main structural components are there. They have three different form languages that are carried out across their designs that follow their functions. For example, the one on the left is modern and angular. The middle one is more conventional and expresses versatility. The right one expresses fragility and delicateness.
